--- ## Skort #damn-girl!
Thoughts: I am surprised at how this one turned out. Besides loving the fabric, this was my first go at using a store-bought pattern.

What’s New: I picked up the pattern months prior to actually getting the sewing done. Execution took a long while!

Tips: Preserve patterns with freezer paper. Making a muslin pays off. Adjusting for fit is a learning process!

Notes for next time: Cute fabric, but not ideal for this garment. Watch for wear in high-friction areas.

--- ## Trip to Mood Fabrics #worth-the-hype?
Thoughts: Walking through so many fabric tubes and samples is overwhelming! Mood Fabrics has been on my bucket list since Project Runway.

What’s New: The interest to start sewing and crafting erupted after seeing folks post their process online.

Notes for Next Time: Physical shopping is fun but overwhelming. Use sample books and recommended fabrics for less stress.

--- ## Setting Up a Projector for Sewing #exciting
Thoughts: This makes me feel so cool, even if I haven’t used it much yet!

What’s New: Experience with printed and store-bought patterns made projection appealing.

Notes for Next Time: I’ll likely project onto paper first, then cut and pin. Sharing digital patterns builds community!

--- ## Sewing a Pin Cushion #cute-af
Thoughts: The cutest thing, and so useful.

What’s New: Magnetic pin holders aren’t enough. Scraps make great filling!

Notes for Next Time: Make a tailor’s ham next. Save money, make it cute!

--- ## Outdoor Sofa Cushion Covers #damn-girl! #proud
Thoughts: Wow. I did that. This project involved observation, research, and just trying things.

What’s New: Tasked to create covers, I hand-drafted patterns and chose materials for durability.

Notes for Next Time: I rock. This reminded me why I’m an engineer—curiosity and tenacity achieve goals!

--- ## Mending Worn Out Pants
Thoughts: I found a local class in decorative mending and loved meeting fellow crafters.

What’s New: Tried visible mending and Sashiko techniques. Planning takes longer than mending, but the results are worth it.

---

What a summer! I’ve learned tons and can’t wait for the next project. Sewing for myself and as gifts is the best.
